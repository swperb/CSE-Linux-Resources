CSE 3724: Computer Organization

Computer Organization is a course designed to introduce students to the physical architecure of a computer, how it thinks and reasons, as well as how it processes human input. In this course, students will learn about the underlying circuitry of a Central Processing Unit (CPU), the processing and understanding of binary data, and the logical circuitry made up by the physical components of a computer. The students will have the opportunity to program an actual CPU through the Assembly language, as well as learn how high-level programming languages are processed down to machine code.

About

As the creator of this website, my vision was to enhance the learning experience for CSE students here at MSU. The goal was and still is to fill in the gaps left from classes due to strict college schedules and offer supplemental material to enhance students' performance, not only in the classroom, but in personal programming experiences as well.
